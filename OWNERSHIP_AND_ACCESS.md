# Ownership and Access

## Principle

The approved family steward is the initial project steward. Access should protect the family legacy, the mission, and the people involved.

No unauthorized ownership transfer should happen. No one should be able to quietly take control, change the mission, or publish the project without documented approval.

Contributors, sponsors, designers, advisors, and helpers do not automatically own the project, website, name, records, or decision authority.

## Recommended Access Roles

- Approved family steward: verifies launch readiness and approves major changes.
- Website maintainer: updates approved content only.
- Archive editor: helps add approved stories, photos, and tournament history.
- Contact manager: updates approved contact details only.
- Sponsor verifier: confirms sponsor names and permissions.

## Before Public Launch

Confirm:

- Who owns the website account.
- Who can deploy public changes.
- Who can edit contact details.
- Who can receive submissions, if any.
- Who can export records.
- How access is removed when someone steps away.

## Access Rules

- Access should be role-based.
- Credentials and access must be protected.
- Use strong unique passwords.
- Enable multi-factor authentication where available.
- Avoid shared personal logins.
- Keep a written access list.
- Review access after major decisions or role changes.
- Remove access promptly when no longer needed.

## Audit Log

Major access changes should be logged with date, person, reason, approver, and action taken.

## Emergency Lockdown Process

Use this process if ownership, privacy, or public launch control appears compromised:

1. Pause all public announcements and sponsor outreach.
2. Remove or disable public forms and unapproved links.
3. Preserve current records, screenshots, logs, and decision history.
4. Change passwords and review two-factor authentication.
5. Remove unapproved account access.
6. Notify the approved family steward.
7. Document what happened, who reviewed it, and what corrective action was taken.
8. Do not resume public activity until approval is confirmed in writing.
